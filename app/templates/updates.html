The Latest Updates and News on the Roundup Orthology Database

150 Genomes Added

By dennis on June 29th, 2009

Some great news: the most recent run of computation has finished after many months. The data from 150 new genomes are now available to roundup users. This brings us up to a total of 399. New computation is under way for a remaining 500 genomes, though this will take many months.

About 790 genomes currently being computed

By dennis on June 8th, 2009

This amounts to about 6.4 gig of genomic data to compute, although the results files will be much, much larger...

Last Friday's outage

By dennis on June 8th, 2009

Apologies to all for the outage on Friday 5 June. We hope it didn't inconvenience any research. The problem was caused by some older versions of code being uploaded to the new site. Roundup was running again by early Saturday 6 June. once again, our sincerest apologies to our users.

Just a few more things

By dennis on April 22nd, 2009

I'm completing the new portal on the front end, while Todd works on various database improvements to improve performance and speed of computation.

Please be patient with us as things look a little flakey over the next few days, everything should be okay by the weekend.

The New Roundup Interface

By dennis on April 11th, 2009

I've been hard at work making the Roundup portal more usable and informative to users.

There is a lot of back-end work being done by Todd Deluca geared towards improved performance, but I'll let him describe his activities here. Meanwhile Parul is doing some amazing work experimenting with heavy duty computation on Amazon's Elastic Cloud.

A lot of work background work is coming to fruition, though it's been less less visible to users for the past twelve months. So I'm adding to Roundup a microbloggins system where you can learn more about what we are doing with Roundup, and how we're doing it. More to come on this.
